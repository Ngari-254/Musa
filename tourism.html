About Tourism

Malindi is a beautiful coastal town in Kenya, located on the Indian Ocean. Known for its pristine beaches, rich history, and vibrant culture, Malindi is a must-visit destination for tourists seeking relaxation and adventure.

Malindi Marine National Park

Highlights: Coral reefs, marine life, snorkeling, and diving.

Description: Kenya first marine park, known for its rich biodiversity. Visitors can explore the underwater world through snorkeling, scuba diving, or glass-bottom boat tours.

Vasco da Gama Pillar

Highlights: Historical monument, ocean views.

Description: Erected in 1498 by the Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama, this pillar is one of the oldest European monuments in Africa and offers a glimpse into Malindi’s historical significance.

Gedi Ruins

Highlights: Ancient Swahili architecture, archaeological site.

Description: Located in a forested area, these ruins are remnants of a once-thriving 13th-century Swahili town. The site includes a palace, mosques, and homes, providing insight into the region's past.

Watamu Beach

Highlights: White sandy beaches, clear waters.

Description: Just a short drive from Malindi, Watamu Beach is renowned for its pristine beauty. It's perfect for relaxation, swimming, and exploring the nearby Watamu Marine National Park.

Falconry of Kenya

Highlights: Birds of prey, educational tours.

Description: This attraction offers visitors the chance to learn about falconry and see a variety of birds of prey up close. It's an educational and interactive experience.

Mida Creek

Highlights: Mangrove forests, birdwatching, boat tours.

Description: Mida Creek is an important ecological site with extensive mangrove forests and diverse bird species. Visitors can explore the creek by boat or enjoy a tranquil walk along its boardwalk.

Malindi Beach

Highlights: Relaxation, swimming, beach sports.

Description: The main beach in Malindi is a favorite spot for tourists and locals alike, offering opportunities for various water sports, sunbathing, and enjoying the coastal vibe.

Marafa Depression (Hell's Kitchen)

Highlights: Geological formations, sunset views.

Description: Known locally as Hell’s Kitchen, this natural sandstone formation is famous for its striking red, orange, and pink hues. The site is especially breathtaking at sunset.

Bio-Ken Snake Farm

Highlights: Snake conservation, educational tours.

Description: This farm is dedicated to the conservation and study of snakes. Visitors can learn about various snake species and their roles in the ecosystem.

Arabuko-Sokoke Forest

Highlights: Biodiversity, birdwatching, hiking.

Description: This is the largest remaining coastal forest in East Africa, home to rare species of birds and mammals. The forest offers nature trails and birdwatching opportunities for visitors.

The House of Columns

Highlights: Historical architecture, cultural exhibits.

Description: This historical building in Malindi showcases Swahili architecture and houses exhibitions that tell the story of the town’s rich cultural heritage.

Malindi Museum

Highlights: Local history, cultural artifacts.

Description: Located in a historic building, the Malindi Museum offers insights into the town's past, showcasing artifacts and exhibits related to the region’s Swahili culture and Portuguese influence.
